The Universe

What Is The Universe?

The Universe is all of space and spacetime and its contents, including planets, moons, stars, galaxies, intergalactic space, and all matter and energy. While the total size of the Universe is unknown, the observable Universe can be measured.

Beginning of the Universe

The Big Bang theory is the leading cosmological model describing the Universe's evolution. Space and time emerged together approximately 13.8 billion years ago, with an initial state of extreme heat and density. As the Universe expanded, it cooled, allowing subatomic particles and atoms to form, leading to galaxies, stars, and planets.

Big Bang

The Big Bang theory suggests the Universe started from an incredibly hot and dense state, expanding over billions of years. It explains the redshift of galaxies, the cosmic microwave background radiation, and the abundance of light elements. The Lambda-CDM model, a refined version, provides a comprehensive explanation of observations about the Universe.
