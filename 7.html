GALAXY

A galaxy is a gravitationally bound system of stars, stellar remnants, interstellar gas, dust, and dark matter.

The word galaxy is derived from the Greek 'galaxias,' meaning "milky," a reference to the Milky Way.

Galaxies range in size from dwarfs with a few hundred million stars to giants with one hundred trillion stars.

OUR MILKY WAY GALAXY

The Milky Way is the galaxy that contains our Solar System.

The descriptive "milky" comes from its appearance as a band of light in the night sky.

Until the 1920s, astronomers believed the Milky Way contained all the stars in the Universe.
